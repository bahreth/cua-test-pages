Abstract

This paper presents a comprehensive analysis of global temperature patterns observed between 2020 and 2024. Using data from 847 monitoring stations across six continents, we identify significant trends in seasonal temperature variations and their correlation with atmospheric carbon dioxide levels. Our findings suggest an acceleration in warming patterns, particularly in polar regions, with implications for global climate policy.

1. Introduction

Climate change remains one of the most pressing challenges facing humanity. This study builds upon previous research conducted by the International Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) and extends the analysis using newly available satellite data and ground-based measurements.

2. Methodology

Data was collected from the Global Historical Climatology Network (GHCN) and processed using standard anomaly detection algorithms. Temperature readings were normalized against the 1951-1980 baseline period to ensure consistency with established climate research protocols.
